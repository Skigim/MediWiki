# Qualified Non-Citizen Status Eligibility Guide

**Nebraska Department of Health and Human Services | Medicaid & Long-Term Care**

## Overview

This guide provides comprehensive information on qualified non-citizen immigration statuses that may make individuals eligible for Medicaid. It details verification requirements, documentation needed, and specific criteria for each immigration category. The guide is designed to assist workers in determining whether a qualified alien is eligible for benefits.

**Last Updated:** February 2024

## Key Definitions

**Qualified Non-Citizen:** An individual who is lawfully admitted to the United States and meets specific criteria outlined in federal law. Qualified non-citizens may be eligible for Medicaid benefits.

**Lawful Permanent Resident (LPR):** An individual who has been granted authorization to live and work in the United States on a permanent basis. Often referred to as having a "green card."

**Five-Year Bar:** A waiting period requiring many qualified non-citizens to reside in the U.S. with lawful permanent resident status for at least five years before becoming eligible for Medicaid (exceptions apply).

**Refugee Medical Assistance (RMA):** A program administered by Economic Assistance providing medical coverage to refugees, asylees, and certain other qualified non-citizens who are ineligible for Medicaid and are within their first 12 months of entry (if entry date is 10/1/2021 or later).

**USCIS:** United States Citizenship and Immigration Services - the federal agency that oversees lawful immigration to the United States.

**Class of Admission (COA):** A code on immigration documents indicating the category under which an individual was admitted to the United States.

## General Eligibility Criteria

Individuals may be eligible if they were lawfully admitted to the United States and meet one of the following criteria:

### Primary Criteria

1. **Age 65 or older** and was lawfully residing in the U.S. on 08-22-96

2. **Had citizenship verified** through SSI, SSDI, or are/were entitled to Medicare benefits

3. **Under age 19 or pregnant**

4. **Resided in the U.S. for at least five years** with lawful permanent resident status

5. **Has lawful permanent resident status** and has worked or can be credited with 40 qualifying quarters of work

6. **If none of the criteria in 1-5 is met**, the individual must meet one of the criteria listed in the sections below to be considered eligible

## Refugee Entrants

**Eligibility Status:** Considered qualified aliens **indefinitely**

### Verification Documents

Refugee status is determined by the following:

1. **USCIS Form I-94** showing entry as refugee under Section 207
2. **USCIS Form I-688B** annotated 274a.12(a)(3)
3. **USCIS Form I-766** annotated A3
4. **USCIS Form I-571**
5. **USCIS Form I-551 or I-151** annotated RE1, RE2, RE3, RE4, RE5, RE6, RE7, RE8, RE8b, RE9, IC6, or IC7

**Important Note about Status Updates:**
- RE-6, RE-7, RE-8, or RE-9 codes show that the individual's status was updated from refugee to lawful permanent resident
- **Even if this status update occurs, the individual remains eligible**

### Refugee Medical Assistance

If ineligible for Medicaid, refugees admitted under Section 207 may be eligible for Refugee Medical Assistance through Economic Assistance if within the first 12 months of entry (if entry date is 10/1/2021 or later).

## Asylee Entrants

**Eligibility Status:** Considered qualified aliens **indefinitely**

### Verification Documents

Asylee status is determined by the following:

1. **USCIS Form I-94** showing grant of asylum under Section 208
2. **Grant/approval letter** from Asylum Office of the USCIS
3. **Order from immigration judge** granting asylum
4. **USCIS Form I-688B** annotated 274.a12(a)(5) annotated at AO3, AO4, or AO5
5. **USCIS Form I-766** annotated A5, AS-1, AS-2, or AS-3
6. **USCIS Form I-551 or I-151** annotated with AS6, AS7, AS8, AS9, GA6, GA7, or GA8

**Important Note about Status Updates:**
- These codes show that the individual's status was updated from asylee to lawful permanent resident
- **Even if this status update occurs, the individual remains eligible**

### Refugee Medical Assistance

If ineligible for Medicaid, asylees admitted under Section 208 may be eligible for Refugee Medical Assistance through Economic Assistance if within the first 12 months of entry (if entry date is 10/1/2021 or later).

---

## Parolee Entrants

**Eligibility Status:** Considered eligible aliens if admitted under Section 212(d)(5) of the Immigration and Nationality Act (INA), the parolee status is granted for **at least one year**, and the individual meets additional criteria

### Verification and Criteria

1. **Has USCIS Form I-94** showing admission under section 212(d)(5) of the INA, AND

2. **Meets military eligibility criteria** identified under section K (see Other Qualified Immigration Statuses section); OR

3. **Has resided in the U.S. for at least 5 years** with parolee status; OR

4. **Is from Afghanistan** with a status grant date of July 31, 2021 or later under Operation Allies Refuge, with code DT, PAR, or OAR
   - These individuals are exempt from the 5-year bar through March 31, 2023 or the term of the parole, whichever is later
   - If no Form I-94 available, may provide a foreign passport with Department of Homeland Security/Customs and Border Patrol admission stamp noting OAR or OAW

5. **Is an individual from Ukraine** with a status grant date of February 24, 2022 or later with code DT, PAR, or UHP
   - If no Form I-94 available, may provide a foreign passport with Department of Homeland Security/Customs and Border Patrol admission stamp noting DT, PAR, or UHP

### Refugee Medical Assistance

If ineligible for Medicaid, parolees admitted as asylees or refugees under Section 212(d), Ukrainian humanitarian parolees, or Afghan parolees admitted under Operation Allies Refuge may be eligible for Refugee Medical Assistance through Economic Assistance if within the first 12 months of entry (if entry date is 10/1/2021 or later).

## Other Qualified Immigration Statuses

### D. Withheld Deportation

**Eligibility Status:** Considered qualified aliens **indefinitely**

**Verification:**
1. Order from Immigration Judge showing deportation withheld under Section 243(h) or 241(b)(3)
2. USCIS Form I-688B annotated 274a.12(a)(10)
3. USCIS Form I-766 annotated A-10
4. If the individual's status is updated to lawful permanent resident, the individual remains eligible

---

### E. Cuban or Haitian Entrants

**Eligibility Status:** Considered qualified aliens **indefinitely**

**Verification:**
1. USCIS Form I-94 annotated with code CU6 or CU7, or stamped Cuban/Haitian Entrant under Section 212(d)(5) of the INA
2. Unexpired temporary I-551 stamp in foreign passport, USCIS Form I-551, or I-151 annotated with code CU6, CU7, CH6, HA6 or HB6
   - These codes show that the individual's status was updated from Cuban/Haitian Entrant to lawful permanent resident. Even if this occurs, the individual remains eligible
3. I-688B with provision of law 274a.12(a)(4)/(10) or 274a.12(c)(8)/(10)/(11)
4. I-766 with code AO4, A10, CO10 or C11
5. Is subject of removal proceedings (240 hearing)
6. Has an application for asylum pending

**RMA Eligibility:** If ineligible for Medicaid, Cuban and Haitian entrants may be eligible for Refugee Medical Assistance through Economic Assistance if within the first 12 months of entry (if entry date is 10/1/2021 or later).

---

### F. Amerasian Entrants

**Eligibility Status:** Considered eligible aliens **indefinitely**

**Verification:**
1. USCIS Form I-94 annotated with code AM1, AM2, or AM3
2. Unexpired temporary I-551 stamp in foreign passport or USCIS Form I-551 annotated with code AM6, AM7, or AM8
   - These codes show that the individual's status was updated from Amerasian entrant to lawful permanent resident. Even if this occurs, the individual remains eligible

**RMA Eligibility:** If ineligible for Medicaid, Amerasians may be eligible for Refugee Medical Assistance through Economic Assistance if within the first 12 months of entry (if entry date is 10/1/2021 or later).

---

### G. Victims of Human Trafficking

**Eligibility Status:** Considered qualified aliens as long as their certification or eligibility letter is valid

**Verification:**

**For individuals age 18 or older:**
- Must have an original certification letter issued from the Office of Refugee Resettlement (ORR)
- The date of entry is the date listed in the body of the letter

**For individuals younger than 18:**
- Must have an original eligibility letter issued from ORR
- The date of entry is the date listed in the body of the letter

**Alternative Verification:**
- USCIS Form I-94 showing entry under section 212(d)(5) of the INA
  - Eligible if status granted for at least one year
  - Visa annotated T-1, T-2, T-3, T-4, T-5 or T-6

**RMA Eligibility:** If ineligible for Medicaid, victims of a severe form of trafficking may be eligible for Refugee Medical Assistance through Economic Assistance if within the first 12 months of entry (if entry date is 10/1/2021 or later).

---

### H. Conditional Entry

**Eligibility Status:** Individuals granted conditional entry pursuant to USCIS Section 203(a)(7) are considered qualified aliens if the individual meets specific criteria

**Criteria:**
1. Meets military eligibility criteria identified under Section L below; OR
2. Has resided in the U.S. for at least 5 years with conditional entrant status; OR
3. Has USCIS Form I-668B annotated 274a.12(a)(3); OR
4. Has USCIS Form I-766 annotated A3; OR
5. Has USCIS Form 1-551 or I-151 with code R86
   - This code shows that the individual's status was updated from conditional entrant to lawful permanent resident
   - Eligibility may also be granted with this code if the individual meets qualifying earned income credit criteria

**RMA Eligibility:** If ineligible for Medicaid, individuals granted conditional entry under USCIS section 203(a)(7) may be eligible for Refugee Medical Assistance through Economic Assistance if within the first 12 months of entry (if entry date is 10/1/2021 or later).

---

### I. Battered Spouses and/or Children

**Eligibility Status:** Considered qualified aliens if the individual has petitioned USCIS for permanent resident status

**Criteria:**
1. Has resided in the U.S. 5 years with USCIS status; OR
2. Meets the military criteria identified in Section L below; OR
3. Has worked or can be credited with 40 qualifying quarters of work

**Additional Requirement:**
- The individual must **NOT** be residing with the family member who battered them

**Verification:**
- I-551 annotated with IB6, IB7, or IB8
- USCIS form I-797 when this form is a Notice of Approval or Notice of Prima Facie Determination
- Other USCIS documentation of battered status (such as an approved self-petition, cancellation of removal, or suspension of deportation filed as a victim of domestic violence)

---

### J. Aliens with Past or Current U.S. Military Involvement

**Eligibility Status:** Considered eligible aliens

**Past or current military involvement is defined as:**

**1. Active Duty:**
- An individual who is on active duty with any of the U.S. Armed Forces branches
- Active duty is defined as minimum active duty (24 months), or the period for which the individual was called to active duty
- **If the active duty is for training** such as National Guard or Army Reserve, the individual is **NOT** eligible

**2. Honorably Discharged or Died in Service:**
- An individual was honorably discharged from the U.S. Armed Forces for reasons other than his/her alien status (as long as they fulfill the minimum active duty service), or has died in active military, naval, or air service
- This includes an individual who served before July 1, 1946 in the organized military forces of the Government of the Commonwealth of the Philippines while such forces were in the service of the Armed Forces of the U.S. or in the Philippine Scouts
- **If the individual was dishonorably discharged**, they are **NOT** eligible under this status

---

### K. Spouses or Unmarried Dependent Children of Military Members

**Eligibility Status:** Considered eligible aliens if specific criteria are met

**Spouse Criteria:**
1. The spouse must currently be married to the veteran/service (wo)man
2. If the veteran/service (wo)man is deceased, the spouse must have been married to him/her at the time of death for at least one year, or for any period of time if a child was born during the marriage
3. The spouse has not remarried

**Important Notes for Spouses:**
- The spouse would NOT be eligible if divorced from the veteran/service (wo)man or has remarried since the veteran/service (wo)man's death
- The spouse may be eligible regardless of whether or not the veteran/service (wo)man is a U.S. citizen
- Eligibility ends if the service (wo)man is discharged from active duty for alien status or dishonorable service

**Unmarried Dependent Children Criteria:**
- A legally adopted or biological dependent child of an honorably discharged veteran, active duty member of the Armed Forces, or deceased veteran
- The child must be under the age of 18 or a full-time student under the age of 22; OR
- A disabled child age 18 or older (if the child was disabled and dependent on the active duty member or veteran prior to the child's 18th birthday)

**Important Notes for Children:**
- The child does not have to be residing with veteran/service (wo)man
- Eligibility ends if the child marries, is no longer considered a dependent, or the service (wo)man is discharged from active duty for alien status or dishonorable service

---

### L. Iraqi Special Immigrants

**Eligibility Status:** Considered eligible aliens from their date of entry or from the date their status was updated to a special immigrant status

**Family Members:**
- Spouses and unmarried children (under the age of 21) of Iraqi special immigrants are eligible from their date of entry or from the date their status was updated to a special immigrant status

**Status Continuation Rule:**
- If the individual entered the U.S. under a different immigration status and is eligible under program rules with that status, then that status will continue regardless of whether or not the individual changes their status to an Iraqi special immigrant status

**Example:** Asylees are eligible without time restrictions, so an individual with asylee status remains eligible under this status even if the individual updates their status to Iraqi special immigrant at a later date.

**Verification:**
1. An Iraqi passport noting admittance under IV (immigrant visa) category SI1, SQ1, SI2, SQ2, SI3, or SQ3 and DHS stamp, notation, or I-94 showing a date of entry or a date of status granted; OR
2. DHS Form 551 (green card) noting Iraqi nationality (or Iraqi passport) with an IV (immigrant visa) code of SI6, SQ6, SI7, SQ7, SI9, SQ9, or SIV

**RMA Eligibility:** If ineligible for Medicaid, Iraqi Special Immigrants may be eligible for Refugee Medical Assistance through Economic Assistance if within the first 12 months of entry (if entry date is 10/1/2021 or later).

---

### M. Afghan Special Immigrants

**Eligibility Status:** Considered eligible aliens from their date of entry or from the date their status was updated to a special immigrant status

**Family Members:**
- Spouses and unmarried children (under the age of 21) of Afghan special immigrants are eligible from their date of entry or from the date their status was updated to a special immigrant status

**Special Immigrant Statuses Include:**
- Special Immigrant LPR
- Special Immigrant Parolee
- Special Immigrant Conditional Permanent Resident (CPR)

**Status Continuation Rule:**
- If the individual entered the U.S. under a different immigration status and is eligible under program rules with that status, then that status will continue regardless of whether or not the individual changes their status to an Afghan special immigrant status

**Verification:**
1. An Afghan passport noting admittance under IV (immigrant visa) category SI1, SQ1, SI2, SQ2, SI3, SQ3, SQ4, SQ5, CQ1, CQ2 or CQ3, and DHS stamp, or notation or I-94 showing a date of entry or a date of status granted; OR
2. DHS Form 551 (green card) noting Afghan nationality (or Afghan passport) with an IV (immigrant visa) code of SI6, SQ6, SI7, SQ7, SI8, SQ8, SI9, SQ9, or SIV

**RMA Eligibility:** If ineligible for Medicaid, Afghan Special Immigrants may be eligible for Refugee Medical Assistance through Economic Assistance if within the first 12 months of entry (if entry date is 10/1/2021 or later).

### N. Native Americans

**Eligibility Status:** Meet eligible alien status **indefinitely**

**Criteria 1: Born in Canada**
- The individual must possess at least 50% blood of the American Indian Race
- Verification may be obtained from birth records, affidavits from tribal officials, USCIS Form I-181 or I-551, or other acceptable documentation
- I-551 may be annotated with KIC, KIP, or S13

**Criteria 2: Member of American Indian Tribe**
- Individual was born outside the United States, however they are a member of an American Indian Tribe
- A state Office may be contacted to make a determination on the American Indian determination
- Verification may be obtained from birth records, affidavits from tribal officials, USCIS Form I-181 or I-551, or other acceptable documentation
- I-551 may be annotated with KIC, KIP, or S13

---

### O. Compacts of Free Association (COFA)

**Eligibility Status:** Qualified non-citizens as defined in section 431(b) of the Personal Responsibility and Work Opportunity Reconciliation Act (PRWORA) and are **exempt from the 5-year bar**

**Eligible COFA Migrants:**
- Citizens of Palau, the Marshall Islands, and the Federated States of Micronesia living in United States through treaties

**Class of Admission (COA) Codes:**
- CFA - Compact of Free Association - Palau, Micronesia, Marshall Islands
- FAS - Citizen of the Freely Associated States
- RMI - Citizen of the Republic of the Marshall Island
- MIS - Citizen of the Republic of Marshall Islands
- PAL - Citizen of the Republic of Palau
- FSM - Citizen of the Federated States of Micronesia

**Important Note:**
- If the individual's status is updated to lawful permanent resident, the individual **no longer remains eligible as a qualified non-citizen**

**Additional Resources:**
- See the Compacts of Free Association (COFA) Policy Memo 21-04 for additional information
- Verify Lawful Presence (VLP) has since been updated in NFOCUS to verify these individuals as qualified non-citizens
- For more information about COFA migrants who update their status to lawful permanent resident, see 5-Year Bar QA

## Key Takeaways

✓ **Refugees, asylees, and certain other qualified non-citizens remain eligible indefinitely** - Even when status is updated to lawful permanent resident

✓ **Afghan and Ukrainian humanitarian parolees exempt from 5-year bar** - Afghan parolees (grant date 7/31/2021 or later) exempt through 3/31/2023 or term of parole; Ukrainian parolees (grant date 2/24/2022 or later) also exempt

✓ **COFA migrants are exempt from the 5-year bar** - Citizens of Palau, Marshall Islands, and Federated States of Micronesia are qualified non-citizens (unless status updated to LPR)

✓ **Victims of trafficking verified through ORR letters** - Adults need certification letter, children under 18 need eligibility letter (date in body of letter is entry date)

✓ **Military involvement extends eligibility** - Active duty, honorably discharged veterans, and their spouses/unmarried dependent children are considered eligible aliens

✓ **Battered spouses/children must not reside with batterer** - Must have petitioned USCIS for permanent resident status and meet additional criteria (5-year residence, military criteria, or 40 qualifying quarters)

✓ **Iraqi and Afghan Special Immigrants eligible from entry/status date** - Includes spouses and unmarried children under age 21; special statuses include LPR, Parolee, and CPR

✓ **Refugee Medical Assistance available for first 12 months** - If ineligible for Medicaid and entry date is 10/1/2021 or later, may qualify for RMA through Economic Assistance

✓ **These codes are not all-inclusive** - Codes are added and changed regularly; send questions to DHHS.MedicaidPolicyQuestions@nebraska.gov

✓ **Document verification is status-specific** - Each immigration category has specific USCIS forms and codes that verify eligible status
